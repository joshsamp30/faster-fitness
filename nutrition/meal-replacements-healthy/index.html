What Are The Best Meal Replacements?

Whole food should do most of the work, but a practical shake or bar can help you avoid missing meals or grabbing something that pulls you off track.

By Marshall RayOriginally published 2014-04-17Updated 2026-05-315 min read

Use As A Backup

Meal replacements are useful when they protect consistency, not when they replace learning how to eat.

Meal replacements can help when life is busy, but they work best as a backup plan, not the foundation. Here is how to choose smarter options.

Whole Food Comes First

Meals built from protein, vegetables, carbs, and healthy fats should be the foundation. They are more filling, more flexible, and easier to adjust based on your goal.

Shakes Can Be Useful

A protein shake can help when you are short on time, finishing a workout, or struggling to hit protein. Look for a product that is simple, high-quality, and easy to digest.

Bars Are A Backup

Bars can be convenient, but many are closer to candy than a meal. Check protein, fiber, added sugar, and ingredients. Use bars when they solve a real schedule problem, not as a daily crutch.

Use Them To Stay Consistent

The best meal replacement is the one that keeps you from skipping food or making a rushed choice you regret. It should support the plan, not replace learning how to eat.

Ask Your Coach

If you train at Faster Fitness, bring the question to your coach. The right answer depends on your schedule, appetite, goals, and current habits.

How Faster Fitness Applies This

Coaches help members decide when a shake, bar, or full meal makes sense for their training and schedule.
